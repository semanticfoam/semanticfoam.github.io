Semantic Foam
Unifying Spatial and Semantic Scene Decomposition

Abstract

Modern scene reconstruction methods such as 3D Gaussian Splatting enable photo-realistic novel view synthesis at real-time speeds, yet their adoption in interactive graphics applications remains limited. A key bottleneck is the difficulty of interacting with these representations compared to traditional, human-authored 3D assets.

We introduce Semantic Foam, extending the recently proposed Radiant Foam representation to semantic decomposition. Our approach combines a volumetric Voronoi mesh with an explicit semantic feature field defined at the cell level, enabling direct spatial regularization and improving consistency across views.

Our method achieves improved object-level segmentation quality compared to prior approaches such as Gaussian Grouping and SAGA.
